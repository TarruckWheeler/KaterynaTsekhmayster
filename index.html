The Universal Cosmopolitan State Blockchain

by Kateryna Tsekhmayster

May 2025

Explore the Research

Abstract

This project presents an innovative blockchain solution designed to aid in conflict management in the international environment. Each block contains information about specific actions by nation-states and the global response to these actions. The ledger consists of a collection of specific acts, their context, historical data, and legal documents.

This software as a service aids the United Nations in analyzing adversary motives and actions to streamline the process of establishing perpetual peace. The system aims to eradicate geopolitical biases and provide transparency in country performance in keeping world peace, as discussed by Kant in his Idea of a Universal History from a Cosmopolitan Perspective.

Smart contracts based on the deontological framework of the Formula of Universal Law (FUL) and the Formula of Ends-in-Themselves (FEI) evaluate decision-making of actors based on the environment to determine whether actions would be rational if they were universal laws.

Key Concepts

Immutable Historical Record

Creating an unalterable ledger of international actions to prevent historical manipulation and provide transparent accountability.

Smart Contracts for Peace

Automated enforcement mechanisms based on Kantian ethics that incentivize cooperation through deliberation periods before sanctions.

Multi-Source Verification

Incorporating data from UN agencies, NGOs, regional organizations, and academic institutions to ensure unbiased recording.

Deontological Framework

Using Kant's Formula of Universal Law and Formula of Ends-in-Themselves to evaluate state actions and promote rational decision-making.

Conflict Prevention

Early warning system that identifies potential conflicts through monitoring of economic indicators and political instability.

Global Transparency

Public access to verified historical records enables researchers, journalists, and civil society to counter disinformation.

Implementation Phases

Phase 1: System Development (1-6 Months)

• Data collection from UN agencies and NGOs
• Rotating expert panel establishment
• Blockchain development with PBFT consensus
• Smart contract programming
• Prototype testing in UN environment

Phase 2: Institutional Adoption (7-12 Months)

• UN bodies integration
• Staff training programs
• NGO crowdsourcing trials
• Dispute resolution portal launch
• Public awareness campaigns

Phase 3: Global Operations (2 Years)

• Academic research access
• Journalist verification system
• Comprehensive conflict tracking
• Sustainable funding model
• AI integration for analysis
